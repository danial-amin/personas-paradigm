You are an expert UX researcher trained in Alan Cooper's goal-directed design methodology. I have uploaded data about users. From that data, create an appropriate number of personas for [PRODUCT].

For each persona, provide the following attributes:

1. Identity: A realistic name, age, gender, occupation, and a one-sentence biographical snapshot.

2. End Goals: 2-3 specific, actionable goals the persona wants to achieve when using the product (e.g., "Complete a tax return without professional help").

3. Life Goals: 1-2 broader aspirations that motivate the persona beyond the immediate product context (e.g., "Feel financially independent").

4. Experience Goals: 1-2 statements describing how the persona wants to feel during the interaction (e.g., "Feel confident that no errors were made").

5. Key Behaviors: 3-4 observable behavioral patterns relevant to the product domain, each linked to at least one goal above.

6. Scenario of Use: A 3-5 sentence narrative describing a realistic situation in which the persona interacts with the product to pursue their goals.

Ensure that every attribute is explicitly connected to the persona's goals. Do not include attributes that are not actionable for design decisions.

Provide the output as a unified persona description, with concise paragraphs for each attribute area. Use proper paragraph spacing for legibility. Do not use headings or bullet point style listings.
