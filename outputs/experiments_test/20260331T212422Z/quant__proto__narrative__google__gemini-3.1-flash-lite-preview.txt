**The Overwhelmed Professional**

This persona is typically a working professional aged 35–44 who spends 7–8 hours online daily. They are often caught in a cycle of digital fatigue, feeling drained and tired after long sessions, yet they struggle with the necessity of being connected for their work. They frequently question the trustworthiness of the content they encounter and feel a sense of guilt or regret regarding their time spent scrolling. They value clear, evidence-based information but feel skeptical about the role of AI in their lives, often worrying about its impact on their job and personal creativity. When they feel overwhelmed, their primary coping mechanism is to take a break or step away from their devices, reflecting a conscious but difficult effort to regain control over their digital wellbeing.

**The Anxious Digital Native**

This persona is typically a younger individual aged 18–34, often balancing work or studies with a high degree of digital immersion, frequently spending 9–12 hours or more online daily. They feel a significant amount of stress and anxiety regarding their digital habits, often describing themselves as "exhausted" or "wasted" after long periods online. They are hyper-aware of the risks of misinformation and feel a high degree of distrust toward big tech companies and AI-generated content. Despite this, they find it difficult to disconnect, often feeling lonely or disconnected after heavy social media use. They are deeply interested in the psychological implications of digital life and are actively seeking tools or strategies to limit their screen time and improve their focus, signaling a strong desire for digital life coaching to help them navigate their relationship with technology.
