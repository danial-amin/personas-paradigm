Alicia Morgan is a 38-year-old female marketing manager in the United States. She works full-time, spends 7–8 hours online most days, and feels tired and mentally drained afterward; she often regrets passive scrolling because it crowds out meaningful time with family, exercise, and rest.

Her end goals are to spend less time on social feeds without losing access to useful information, to quickly verify whether a post or news item is real by checking facts and multiple sources, and to use digital tools intentionally so she can finish work tasks faster and get back to offline life. Her life goals are to feel more in control of her attention and to be a more present parent and partner. Her experience goals are to feel reassured that she is not being manipulated, to feel calmer and less guilty while online, and to feel confident that the time she spends digitally is worthwhile. Her key behaviors are that she frequently looks for multiple sources when something seems questionable, because that supports her goal of verifying truth; she tends to trust academics, experts, and evidence-based content, because that helps her make sound decisions; she takes a break when overwhelmed, because she wants to protect her energy and reduce regret; and she is sensitive to AI-generated or misleading content, because it undermines her verification goal and leaves her feeling annoyed or betrayed. In a realistic use scenario, Alicia opens the coaching app after an evening of scrolling news and social media left her feeling drained and guilty. She sets a goal to cut her nightly screen time by 30 minutes and asks for a quick fact-checking routine she can use before sharing anything. The product helps her compare sources, identify trustworthy evidence, and schedule a break, so she can leave the app feeling calmer and more in control.
