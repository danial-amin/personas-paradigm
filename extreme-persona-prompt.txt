1. Identity & Context: Name, age, and a brief biographical note emphasizing what makes this persona an atypical or extreme user of the product.
 
2. Defining Extreme Characteristic(s): 1-2 specific attributes that place this persona at the edge of the user distribution (e.g., extremely low digital literacy, a rare disability, an unusual use context such as operating the system in a crisis situation, or a marginalized socioeconomic background). Explain why this characteristic is relevant to the product.
 
3. Unique Needs & Requirements: 3-4 needs that arise specifically from the extreme characteristic(s) and that the current design may not accommodate.
 
4. Barriers to Use: 2-3 concrete barriers the persona faces when attempting to use the product, described with enough specificity that a designer could act on them.
 
5. Workarounds & Adaptations: Any current strategies the persona uses to compensate for the barriers above.
 
6. Value to the Design Process: A 2-3 sentence explanation of what designing for this persona would reveal about the system's robustness, accessibility, or inclusivity.
 
7. Scenario of Extreme Use: A 3-5 sentence narrative in which the persona attempts to use the product under challenging or unusual conditions, highlighting breakdowns or unmet needs.
 
Do not default to "average" users. Each persona should challenge assumptions about who uses the product and how. If the product is designed for a mainstream audience, consider users with sensory impairments, cognitive differences, extreme age ranges, low-resource environments, or non-native language contexts.
 
Provide the output as a unified persona description, with concise paragraphs for each attribute area. Use proper paragraph spacing for legibility. Do not use headings or bullet point style listings.
